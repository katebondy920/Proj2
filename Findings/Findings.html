Was LVMH Justified in Backing Out

What Did We Find


Looking at the history of Tiffany & Co. we can track how exactly Tiffany became the American Icon that it is today. LVMH's only other major luxury jewlery company is BVLGARI and while worldwide they are a leader, they do not cary the same household appeal as Tiffany's.

If you look at the number of stores that Tiffany & Co. has in comparison to BVLGARI, you can see how much of an acquisition this would have been for LVMH. Tiffany's has over 90 locations in the US while BVLGARI has only 19.

When you compare LVMH and Tiffany's stock data, at first it appears that they are in line. If anything, LVMH is more volatile and had a bigger drop at the beginning of the pandemic. However, as you move further along in the data, you can see that while LVMH as a whole began to recover, Tiffany & Co. has not been able to make this same recovery.

When you compare the stock data of Tiffany & Co. to that of Pandora Jewlery, a comparable US luxury jelwery company, you see the same thing as when comparing to LVMH. Both stocks follow similar paths at the beginning but pretty soon they diverge and Tiffany continues to turn downward while Pandora starts to go back up.
